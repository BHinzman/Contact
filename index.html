About Me

My journey reads less like a straight line and more like a constellation, each point connected by curiosity and purpose. From ensuring soldiers had the gear they needed to helping startups find their financial footing, I've learned that every complex system, whether military logistics or market dynamics, shares a common truth: the right insight at the right moment changes everything.

I earned my Master's in Management while navigating the unique challenges of defense, finance, and consulting. What stayed constant across these worlds wasn't just the data, but the human need to understand it. That's where I found my calling. Not in the numbers themselves, but in the stories they tell and the decisions they empower.

I've built dashboards that turned $4M budget questions into clear, confident choices. I've designed processes that saved time, reduced waste, and helped leaders sleep better at night. To me, the most beautiful spreadsheet is one that helps someone see what was previously invisible.

When I'm not deep in data, I'm probably deep in thought: about where technology is taking us, what we're becoming as a species, and how we can steer progress toward more meaningful outcomes. I believe in learning not as a task, but as a way of moving through the world.

Projects

Procurement Analytics Dashboard

Leadership was navigating a critical $4M program with limited visibility. They needed to track dozens of delivery timelines, budgets, and vendor relationships, but the data lived in scattered spreadsheets and emails.

I built an interactive dashboard in Excel and Power BI that transformed uncertainty into clarity. Suddenly, decision-makers could see the entire procurement landscape at a glance. The result? Better-aligned budgets, improved on-time deliveries, and the confidence that comes from seeing the whole picture.

Financial Modeling & Market Analysis

Startups came to me with big questions and limited data. They needed to understand their market position, pricing potential, and growth opportunities, but lacked the analytical framework to make informed decisions.

I developed dynamic financial models and market analyses that became their strategic compass. These tools helped them identify hidden opportunities, test scenarios, and build business cases that attracted investment. The five-star reviews and repeat engagements weren't just about the analysis, they were about giving founders the confidence to move forward.

Army Logistics & Inventory Management

Imagine ensuring 20,000 soldiers across 70+ installations have the uniforms and equipment they need, when they need them. The challenge wasn't just scale, it was accuracy, timing, and accountability.

By implementing data-driven processes and analyzing supply patterns, we transformed uniform distribution from a constant challenge into a reliable system. We reduced waste, improved contract compliance, and most importantly, ensured soldiers could focus on their missions rather than their gear. The data didn't just optimize logistics, it supported readiness.

Philosophy

Data as a Human Language

I see data as the universe's way of communicating patterns. My work is about learning to listen, then translating what I hear into something that helps people make better decisions, see new possibilities, and understand their world more deeply.

The Beginner's Mind

True expertise isn't about knowing everything, it's about remaining open to learning anything. I approach each new tool, technology, or challenge with curiosity rather than assumption. The most elegant solutions often come from seeing with fresh eyes.

Technology with Purpose

As innovation accelerates, our humanity must set the direction. I believe in progress that elevates not just capability, but consciousness. The most powerful algorithms are those that help us become more human, not less.

The Connection Principle

My family grounds me; they're the constant in my evolving understanding of legacy and impact. The work I do, the systems I build, the insights I uncover, is ultimately about creating connections that matter, whether between data points or between people.
